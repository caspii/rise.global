The 100 Most Influential Blockchain People

A historical ranking of the 100 most influential individuals shaping the blockchain conversation, originally published on rise.global. Scored on Klout and Twitter signals for the week ending 9 May 2018.

Score period: 2 May – 9 May 2018 Released: 9 May 2018 Top 100 entries archived

This page is a preserved archive of The 100 Most Influential Blockchain People, a leaderboard that ran on rise.global through 2017 and 2018. It tracked the people driving the blockchain conversation each week — founders, investors, journalists, and researchers — blending Klout authority scores with Twitter list inclusion to surface those with both reach and curated relevance.

The list is a snapshot of an extraordinary moment in crypto: Vitalik Buterin at #1, Joseph Lubin at #2, Don Tapscott at #3, with Tim Draper, Andreas M. Antonopoulos, Roger Ver, Charlie Lee, Brock Pierce and Charles Hoskinson all in the top 25. Names that would later become near-mythic in the industry. We are keeping it online because press, academic papers, and industry reports continue to cite it.

About the methodology

The leaderboard was recomputed weekly by rise.global. Each person was given a Power Score derived from Klout (a now-retired social authority metric) and the count of Twitter lists they appeared on, weighted to balance raw reach with curated relevance.

The "Move" column shows the change in rank versus the previous week — a snapshot of momentum within an industry that, in 2018, was moving very fast.

Note: This is a static archive. The data is frozen at the 9 May 2018 release and is no longer updated. The descriptions reflect each person's own Twitter bio at the time. The original URL slug ("top-fintech-people") is retained for backlink continuity, but the leaderboard itself was always titled "The 100 Most Influential Blockchain People".
